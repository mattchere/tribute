Shigeru Miyamoto

The creator of Mario and Zelda

Here's a timeline of Miyamoto's life:

  • 1952 - Born in Sonobe, Kyoto, Japan
  • 1977 - Hired by Nintendo as the company’s first artist
  • 1980 - Helped to develop the game Radar Scope
  • 1981 - Released Donkey Kong
  • 1983 - Released Mario Bros., the first game of the most profitable game series to date
  • 1985 - Released Super Mario Bros., a sequel to the first game
  • 1986 - Released The Legend of Zelda, the first game of the series
  • 1987 - Released The Legend of Zelda II
  • 1993 - Released Star Fox, a key game for the Super Nintendo Entertainment System
  • 1996 - Mentored Satoshi Tajiri to create the highly successful Pokemon game series
  • 1996 - Released Super Mario 64, a Nintendo 64 launch title
  • 1998 - Released The Legend of Zelda: Ocarina of Time, often considered the greatest game of all time
  • 2000 - Released The Legend of Zelda: Majora’s Mask, a critically acclaimed sequel
  • 2001 - Released Pikmin, based on his experience gardening
  • 2002 - Released Metroid Prime in a tribute to his late mentor Yokoi
  • 2004 - Released Metal Gear Solid: The Twin Snakes in conjunction with Hideo Kojima
  • 2004 - Unveiled The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess at E3 2004
  • 2006 - Released the Wii console, of which he had a major role in developing
  • 2006 - Released Wii Sports as a launch title that was successful in engaging non-gamers and gamers alike
  • 2006 - Released The Legend of Zelda: Twilight Princess, the first to use motion-sensors
  • 2007 - Released The Legend of Zelda: Phantom Hourglass, the first to use touch-screen controls
  • 2015 - Assumed the role of acting Representative Director following the death of Nintendo’s president
  • 2015 - Appointed his current role as Creative Fellow at Nintendo
  • 2017 - Released The Legend of Zelda: Breath of the Wild, the latest game in the series
